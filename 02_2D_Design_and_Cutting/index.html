[home](../index.html) # Week 2: 2D Design and Cutting ## A. Parametric Press-Fit Design: <div class="row"> <div class="col-sm-6"> <p style="padding: 0 0em 2em 0;"> Idea:<br> <br> Hexagonal pieces that will fit together to create a variety of different shapes. My favourite shape, the hexagon, inspired me. I was really excited by the idea of assembling them in 3D space and creating all sorts of other 3D shapes derived from the (mostly) 2D hexagons. </p> </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col-sm-6"> <p style="padding: 0 0em 2em 0;"> ------- Initial Sketches:<br> <br> I chose the ‘polygon’ option from the ‘create’ drop-down menu, inputted that I wanted 6 sides, and sketched out the lengths. Then I went into the modify tab and created parameters for the notch length, thickness, and kerf (all of which will change when I actually cut the hexagons, and inputting approximate values now just means that the parameters are already ready for me to change at that time). Then I created a rectangle of the length I had previously set in my parameters for the notch depth and of the width thickness - kerf. I then used the ‘circular pattern’ option, also from the create toolbar, to mirror it around the hexagon 3 times, creating symmetric notches. </p> </div> <div class="col-sm-6"> <img src="IMG_5673.jpeg" alt="initial sketch" style="width:500px;height:250px;"> </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col-sm-6"> <p style="padding: 0 0em 2em 0;"> ------- Extruded Version:<br> <br> Then I extruded the image (by pressing ‘E’), and set the thickness to the thickness parameter I had set previously. Then I added filets (by selecting the filet button and then selecting the inner edges). </p> </div> <div class="col-sm-6"> <img src="IMG_5674.jpeg" alt="extruded version" style="width:500px;height:250px;"> </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col-sm-6"> <p style="padding: 0 0em 2em 0;"> ------- Previewing Assembly:<br> <br> Then I went to see what the 3D version would look like, so I opened it and saw that the notch isn’t quite wide enough to accommodate the second hexagon and that the notches were too deep and wouldn’t allow for more than one hexagon to be properly affixed at once. </p> </div> <div class="col-sm-6"> <img src="IMG_5675.jpeg" alt="assembled view" style="width:500px;height:250px;"> </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col-sm-6"> So, I changed the thickness of the piece and (attempted to) adjusted the notch length to make it slightly better, but resolved to make some test cuts before setting the new measurements in stone. </div> <div class="col-sm-6"> <img src="IMG_5676.jpeg" alt="Second extruded version" style="width:500px;height:250px;"> </div> </div> ------- <div class="row"> <div class="col-sm-6"> <p style="padding: 0 0em 2em 0;"> First Cut: <br> <br> The first cut was mostly a sucess, with my kerf measurements ending up being suprisingly accurate! About a milimeter was an accurate prediction, which I verified. <br> <br> As predicted, the notches were too long and so I traced around to find the ideal length. Then I changed the length in my original file and got ready to recut! <br> <br> To the right are images of the hexagons </p> </div> <div class="col-sm-6"> <img src="IMG_5685.jpeg" alt="side by side" style="width:500px;height:375px;"> <img src="IMG_5686.jpeg" alt="together, with markings" style="width:500px;height:375px;"> </div> </div> ------- ##B. Modelling Household Items <div class="row"> <div class="col-sm-6"> <p style="padding: 0 0em 2em 0;"> Object 1: Eraser<br> <br> I chose the eraser because it is a constant in any workspace and can be effectively modeled in 2D. I intitially created a rectangular sketch, and then used filets of varying sides on either end to match the dimensions of my eraser. To the right are the eraser I was modeling and the model I created, including the parameters (measurements done of the eraser with calipers). </p> </div> <div class="col-sm-6"> <img src="EraserWithParameters.jpeg" alt="eraser 3D model" style="width:500px;height:250px;"> <img src="IMG_5704.jpeg" alt="eraser picture" style="width:500px;height:250px;"> </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col-sm-6"> <p style="padding: 0 em 2em 0;"> ------- Object 2: Gluestick<br> <br> I chose the gluestick because it is composed of different components of geometries altered from the ones created by default. Then I turned each of the bodies into components, at which point I copied and pasted them into a new file and assembled them, as you see in the final image.
